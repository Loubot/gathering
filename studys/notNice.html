(Read Luke 12:51 as a meditation)

'Nice' is one of those words I dislike. I hate to be told I am nice. Some people might say 'nice' meaning something truly nice but most use it to mean something nice and agreeable, always compliant, never challenging or upsetting. I think of a nice person as one who always gives you what you want, - a sort of Santa figure. When it comes to Jesus - he was never nice. He was the opposite of nice - He was loving. He never wanted to be misunderstood as being a nice God for he knew that if we were allowed to think of him as a nice God we would be well on our way to thinking of Jesus as some sort of ATM machine God. Though too many of us, in our immature period, think of Him in just that way.

We sometimes act as if we had God's number; we go to our God outlet and access our ATM machine God with our own personalised access code: P-r-a-y-e-r. We dial-up God with a prayer and we expect a nice God at the other end of the line. This ATM God will give us out what ever we want, whenever we want it, because this is what we expect from a nice God. But Jesus is no Nice God.

I said the reason why so many of us think this way is because we are immature, but this not the full explanation. If this misunderstanding of God is simply a phase we go through then you would expect more Christians to grow out of this immature understanding. But too many don't. Part of the reason why they don't grow-up is because some Pastors who don't want their people to think of God in any other way are deliberately stunting their growth. They preach messages about giving to God X Euro and getting back more than you gave arguing that you can't out give God. And loads of similar junk food is feed to these immature spiritual children by abusive pastoral parents. In many ways these exploitative leaders and the acquiescent children reflected what is going on in wider society. More and more families in the west are made up of parent who can't say a loving 'no' to their kids and kids who demand to be fed on physical, moral and emotional junk.  We give our children what they want rather than what their need in exchange for peace and an easy life. The irony is of course there is no real peace either for the parents of the kids in these families, just over fed kids imprisoned by selfish and unloving parents in a dynamic that will keep the kids immature and dependant.

These parents could never accept that they are anything other than good parents who love their kids dearly. In the end this may be their only excuse. But what excuse do the pastoral parents have when they stand before their God to give account when their defence will be - it made good business sense to have them ignorant of any other aspect of God other than you as an ATM machine God.

They may reason with God that if makes good sense when building a church to have everyone work toward a common easily understood vision. That vision or image is of a God that can be controlled, shaped and presented as a nice God. This avoids the messiness of learning to be with a God as teacher, leader, parent or lover.

One of the reasons why Jesus came as a man and lived among us publicly and privately is to show us he was no nice ATM God. He was a real flesh and blood God: a God who wasn't controlled by our constructs of him. He came as parent who knew what was best for his children. He came as a teacher who corrected us when we got it wrong and he wanted us to be knowledgeable about another kingdom and its dynamic: a kingdom hidden since the foundation of the world. He came as a leader to lead by first going in front of us into the desert, and on to spending time and effort praying for and healing the poor, and ultimately leading us through death. As a leader he asks of those who would follow him to do no more than what he has already done. As a lover he calls us friends; friends that want to be in each other's company. He will not allow his relationship of love with us to be debased by our ignorance, nor will he be acquiescent in our attempts to reshape him into a nice god.

This ATM image of God is not a new creation of our ignorance. It is the construct of the perennial pagan: one who constructs an image of God from his own understanding. It is a vain activity, an activity whose origin is under the sun. God of creation is bigger than what can be dreamt of in our philosophy. Thought we might like to have a nice god, Jesus makes it clear that he is not that god. He is no nice god, reducible to an ATM machine image. In Luke's telling of the Jesus story we are left in no doubt - "Do you think that I have come to bring peace to the earth? No, I tell you, but rather division. From now on 5 will be divided: 3 against 2 and 2 against 3." Lk 12:51…

No ATM God would say such a thing. No ATM machine God could explain saying such a thing. One could only hope for an explanation of such a remark from a great teacher who is a loving parent trying to mature the ones he loves, leading them from ignorance to knowledge of another kingdom.

Jesus is this God or as CS Lewis put it - Jesus leaves you with 2 choices either Jesus is mad or he is God. But one thing is for sure - he is no nice ATM God.
